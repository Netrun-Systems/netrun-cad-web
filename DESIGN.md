## Brand & Style

This is a professional CAD instrument for tablet-first work. Two skins ride on a single application shell: **Netrun CAD — Landscape Design** for designers measuring sites and sketching planting plans on iPad with Apple Pencil, and **Survai Construction** for site teams pulling 3D scan results into a markupable plan view. The visual language is deliberately the same. The mode you're in shows up in branding text and the default toolset, not in the chrome.

The design ethos is **workshop, not boutique**. Surfaces are deep navy, cool, and quiet, so the drawing — the actual content the user is producing — owns the visual energy. There is exactly one warm color in the palette, a coral red, and it is reserved for the *thing you are doing right now*: the active mode, the live tool, the focused stroke. Everything else stays out of the way.

The personality is calm under technical density. AutoCAD LT is the spiritual ancestor: command-line readouts, single-letter shortcuts, and a status bar of monospaced toggles ("GRID ON | SNAP ON | ORTHO OFF") at the foot of the canvas. But the tablet-first ergonomics are 2026: floating glass toolbars over a fullscreen canvas, pressure-sensitive ink, two-finger pan, long-press for context menu. It should feel like a serious tool to a draftsperson who has been using AutoCAD for fifteen years, while remaining inviting to a landscape designer who has never touched a CAD program.

## Colors

The palette is anchored by four navy values stepping from `#0f0f23` (darkest, used for floating chrome) to `#0f3460` (brightest, used for borders and accent fills). Together they form the "deep cool" canvas the application sits in. They were picked to read as ink on dark paper rather than as flat dark mode — there is intentional warmth in the navy tone (a touch of magenta) that prevents the UI from feeling industrial-cold.

Layered on top of the navy is one coral accent, `#e94560`. It carries the entire weight of "this is active." It appears on the selected mode pill, on the focused stroke color swatch, on the snap indicator's outer ring, on the primary CTA on the marketing page, and as the default ink color in Color mode. Using it anywhere else dilutes its meaning, so use restraint.

Status colors follow a small bright palette only used in the status bar and toast-style indicators:

- **Mode active** — a teal `#00d4aa`. Distinct from the coral so a glance at the status bar tells you "what mode" before "what's selected."
- **On / passing** — green `#4ade80` for `GRID ON`, `SNAP ON`, healthy state.
- **Off / blocking** — red `#f87171` for `ORTHO OFF`, errors, destructive actions like Clear All.
- **Prompt / hint** — amber `#ff9800` for the context-aware "click to set first point" italic prompt.

Ink colors (the swatches in CAD/Draw/Color tools) are tuned for legibility on the dark canvas and to evoke real drafting media: bright white for pen, sky blue for blueprint annotation, warm orange for highlight, leaf green for hardscape callouts, coral red for emphasis, and saturated yellow for snap and warning.

For the Survai (construction) marketing landing page, an indigo tertiary `#4f46e5` is used sparingly for secondary CTAs and feature-section accents to differentiate the marketing-mode personality from the in-app coral accent. It is not used inside the canvas application.

Text contrast is pinned to two values: `#e0e0e0` for body and `#7b7b8e` for muted/secondary. Pure white `#ffffff` is reserved for monospace readout values (coordinates, zoom percentage, tool name) where it acts as a visual highlight against the gray label text — not for body copy.

## Typography

The body family is the system stack — `-apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, 'Segoe UI', Roboto, sans-serif` — chosen so the app inherits the operating system's text rendering on iPad, macOS, and Windows without shipping a webfont. On iPad in particular, this means the user sees San Francisco, which the OS already optimizes for the touchscreen display.

A dedicated **monospace stack** (`'Consolas', 'SF Mono', 'Courier New', monospace`) is used for the bottom status bar, the AutoCAD-style command line, and any inline coordinate or numeric readout. The monospace is not stylistic — it is functional. It keeps numbers from jittering as values change ("X 12.34" stays the same width as "X 99.99"), and it visually separates "data the program is reporting" from "controls the user clicks."

The type scale is heavily weighted toward small sizes. The dominant body size is 12-14px because this is a tool with high information density; the canvas is the hero, and the chrome stays small to give it room. Larger sizes (24px, 30px, 44px, 60px) appear only on the marketing landing page hero and section headlines.

Two stylistic tics worth preserving:

- **Branding marks** ("SURVAI" in the toolbar, "Netrun CAD" in the title bar) are rendered in `label-xs` with `letter-spacing: 0.08-0.18em` and uppercase. This is a deliberate echo of engineering drawing title blocks.
- **Italic prompts** in the status bar (in amber) signal "the program is asking you to do something next." This is the only place italics are used.

## Layout & Spacing

The application is **canvas-first**: a fullscreen drawing surface fills the viewport, and everything else floats over it. There is no fixed sidebar layout in the working app. Toolbars are pill-shaped chrome that detach from the corners; side panels slide in from the right edge only when summoned. The status bar is the one piece of always-visible chrome at the foot of the screen, and it is intentionally thin (28px).

Spacing is on a 4px base grid (not 8px) because the floating-toolbar chrome needs sub-8px granularity — a 6px vertical pad on a tool button feels "right" where 8px feels heavy. The 8px scale still dominates content padding in side panels and the marketing landing page; the 4px increments are reserved for compact chrome.

iPad ergonomics inform the layout in three concrete ways:

1. **Safe-area inset** — the application reads `env(safe-area-inset-*)` so chrome avoids the iPad notch in landscape, the home-bar in portrait, and the rounded display corners on iPad Pro.
2. **Two-finger gestures preserved** — pinch-to-zoom and two-finger pan are handled directly by the canvas; the chrome never intercepts them. This is why floating toolbars are pill-shaped rather than full-width — the user can grab the empty area to the side and pan freely.
3. **Touch-friendly targets** — minimum 44px tap targets on iPad, with thumb-on-slider widening from 12px to 20px when the input device is reported as `pointer: coarse`.

Generous interior padding inside side panels (16px) compensates for the otherwise dense chrome. Toolbar buttons are tight (4-8px) because they are clustered into pills where the cluster's bordered glass acts as the visual breathing room.

## Elevation & Depth

Elevation in this design system is conveyed by **darkness, blur, and edge-light**, not by shadow alone:

- **The canvas (level 0)** is the deepest navy `#1a1a2e`. It has no shadow — it IS the floor.
- **Floating toolbars (level 2)** sit visually above the canvas via a darker pill background `rgba(15, 15, 35, 0.95)`, a 4px backdrop blur to subtly diffuse the canvas underneath, and a 1px navy edge that catches just enough light from the implied scene to define the silhouette.
- **Side panels (level 3)** slide in from the right with a stronger drop shadow and a 1px navy left border. The panel does not blur the canvas behind it — it occludes a slice of canvas entirely, giving the user a stable surface to work in without losing canvas context to either side.
- **Modals (level 4)** dim the entire canvas to 70% black with a 4px backdrop blur and float a single bordered card centered over it.
- **Active state (orthogonal)** is communicated by the coral accent glow — `box-shadow: 0 0 0 1px rgba(233, 69, 96, 0.4), 0 6px 18px rgba(233, 69, 96, 0.2)`. This is the only "warm" shadow in the system, and it indicates the tool, mode, or stroke that is currently armed.

Shadows skew darker than typical light-mode systems (opacities 0.3-0.75 instead of 0.1-0.2) because they need to register against an already-dark surface. A 0.1 shadow on `#1a1a2e` is invisible.

There is no glassmorphism in the modern frosted sense. The 4px backdrop blur is functional (it subtly desaturates whatever stroke or geometry sits behind a toolbar so the toolbar text remains legible) rather than decorative.

## Shapes

The shape language is **subtle-rounded** — small enough to read as professional, large enough to read as friendly:

- `rounded-DEFAULT` (4px) — the workhorse for tool buttons, inputs, and tight chrome.
- `rounded-md` (6px) — mode buttons, context menu items.
- `rounded-lg` (8px) — toolbars, side panels, primary CTAs, color-input swatches.
- `rounded-xl` (12px) — modal cards, large status badges.
- `rounded-full` — color swatches in the toolbar, status pills, layer color dots, custom range-slider thumbs.

The marketing landing page uses slightly larger radii (`rounded-2xl`, 16px) on hero cards because it needs to feel inviting rather than instrumental. The in-app chrome stays at the smaller sizes throughout.

Borders are **always 1px**. There is no double-stroke or 2px outline anywhere in the chrome. Active states are signaled by **fill change** (transparent → coral) plus glow, not by a heavier border. The exception is color swatches, which use a 2px white border + 1.10 scale to indicate selection — this works because swatches are small enough that a 1px border would be lost.

## Motion

Motion is fast, short, and ease-out. Two timings cover almost every transition in the app:

- **150ms ease-out** — micro feedback on tool buttons, color swatches, hover states. Fast enough to feel direct on an Apple Pencil tap, slow enough that the user perceives the change rather than seeing it pop.
- **200ms ease-out** — standard transition for mode switches, panel slides, color shifts. The side panel slides open at this duration with both `width` and `transform` animated together.

Two longer durations exist for specific cases:

- **300ms ease-in-out** — modal fade in and out. Backdrop opacity and modal scale animate together.
- **700ms ease-out** — marketing landing page section reveal on scroll. An `IntersectionObserver` flips `opacity-0 translate-y-8` → `opacity-100 translate-y-0` as sections enter the viewport. This is the only place motion exists for delight rather than feedback.

There is no spring physics, no bounce, no oscillation. The app's motion vocabulary is "instrument-grade" — predictable, uniform, and brief. A draftsperson should never have to wait on an animation to do the next thing.

## Components

### Floating toolbars

The signature in-app component. A horizontal pill of `rgba(15, 15, 35, 0.95)`, 1px navy border, 8px radius, 4px backdrop blur, anchored top-center on the canvas. Tool buttons inside it are tight (`px-2 py-1`, `text-xs`, `font-medium`) and arranged with a 4-12px gap depending on cluster density. Vertical hairline dividers (`1px` × `24px` of `cad-accent`) separate functional groupings within a single toolbar. The pill never spans the full canvas width — its width is determined by content, leaving canvas grab area on either side.

### Mode and tool buttons

Two button styles share the same family but signal different scopes. **Mode buttons** (CAD / Draw / Color / Text / Diagram) use `rounded-md`, `text-sm`, and the coral active state with a soft glow. **Tool buttons** within a mode (Line / Rectangle / Circle / Dimension) use `rounded-DEFAULT`, `text-xs`, and a dimmer navy active state without glow. The visual hierarchy reads correctly: mode is more prominent than tool.

### Status bar

A 28px-tall monospace strip at the foot of the canvas. Background is `rgba(26, 26, 46, 0.95)` with a 1px navy top border. Layout is two clusters separated by `flex justify-between`. The left cluster reports state ("X 12.34 Y 8.91 FT | MODE CAD | TOOL LINE | LAYER hardscape | GRID ON | SNAP ON | ORTHO OFF"); the right cluster reports environment ("ZOOM 100% | EL 247") and exposes Reset View / Clear All affordances. Vertical pipe characters (`|` in `#555577`) separate fields. State values use `#ffffff` against `#9ca3af` labels for clear visual separation. ON/OFF flips between green `#4ade80` and red `#f87171`. The active mode shows in teal `#00d4aa` to distinguish it from the coral "selected tool" semantic.

### Command line

A single-line text input that opens at the foot of the canvas above the status bar when the user types or hits Enter. Same monospace stack as the status bar but slightly larger and with a coral caret. Echoes the AutoCAD `Command:` prompt convention. Tab completes registered command aliases; Esc cancels.

### Side panels

Slide in from the right edge at 200ms ease-out. Background is the darkest navy `#0f0f23`, 1px navy left border, 16px interior padding. Section headers inside use the small uppercase `label-xs` in `on-surface-variant` color with an underline of `outline-variant`. List rows (layers, plants, recent projects) are 6px×8px transparent cells with `rounded-md` corners; the active row gets a translucent navy fill `rgba(15, 52, 96, 0.5)`.

### Color swatches and range slider

The CAD toolbar's stroke-color row presents six 20px circular swatches in `rounded-full`. Selected state is a 2px white border and 1.10× scale (no fill change — the swatch *is* the color). The line-width range slider next to it has a 4px navy track and a 12px coral thumb that grows to 20px on touch devices. This is the smallest pattern in the app that captures the whole design philosophy: state is communicated by the coral accent, the track stays cool, and the touch target adapts to the device.

### Context menu

Right-click on desktop, long-press (600ms) on iPad. A small floating card (`min-width: 200px`) with `rounded-lg`, the same dark navy background as side panels, 1px navy border, and the heaviest shadow in the system. Items are `rounded-md` with translucent navy hover.

### Marketing landing page (Survai mode only)

Encountered only on `survai.netrunsystems.com`. Uses a more conventional dark-section layout with the indigo tertiary as a secondary CTA color, larger type scale (44-60px display sizes), `rounded-2xl` hero cards, and the only "delight" motion in the app — the 700ms scroll-reveal fade. Lucide outline icons with 2px stroke replace the canvas's emoji icons throughout. The landing page is a deliberately different visual register from the in-app chrome — it is selling, not working.

### Canvas-internal styling

Not a UI component in the conventional sense, but a critical design surface. The canvas paints its own grid (major lines at 18% opacity navy, minor lines at 8%), its own selection overlay (12% coral fill with a coral stroke), its own snap indicator (a yellow circle), and its own inference lines (50% opacity yellow). These colors live in the same palette but are tuned for "ink on paper" semantics rather than for chrome readability — the canvas is meant to look like a drawing, not like a UI.
